Troubleshooting Reconciliation Mismatches

Most GST reconciliation problems follow a few patterns. Use this checklist to identify the cause and decide the next action before filing GSTR-3B.

Invoice missing from GSTR-2B

Cause: the supplier has not filed GSTR-1 for that period, filed it late, or reported the invoice with incorrect buyer GSTIN.

Fix: contact the supplier, ask them to include or amend the invoice, and claim ITC in the month it appears in GSTR-2B.

Invoice in 2B but not in books

Cause: the supplier reported the invoice but your team has not booked the purchase, or it was booked under a different vendor ledger.

Fix: locate the original invoice, book it in the correct period if valid, and re-run reconciliation.

Invoice number does not match

Cause: prefixes, leading zeros, slashes, or financial-year suffixes are formatted differently in GSTR-2B and Tally.

Fix: compare normalised invoice numbers and review possible matches instead of relying only on exact text matching.

Taxable value or tax amount differs

Cause: rounding, freight, discount treatment, or HSN tax slab differences can create mismatches in taxable value, IGST, CGST, or SGST.

Fix: compare the original invoice with both records. Correct your books if your entry is wrong, or ask the supplier to amend their return if they reported incorrectly.

Column detection problems

Cause: merged cells, blank rows before headers, or non-standard column names may stop automated tools from detecting the right fields.

Fix: keep the header row at the top, unmerge cells, and use clear headings such as GSTIN, Invoice No, Invoice Date, Taxable Value, IGST, CGST, and SGST.
