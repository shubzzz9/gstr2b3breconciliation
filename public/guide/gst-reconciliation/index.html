Complete Guide to GST Reconciliation

GST reconciliation is the monthly process of matching the purchase invoices in your books against GSTR-2B from the GST portal. Correct reconciliation protects Input Tax Credit (ITC), reduces notices, and keeps GSTR-3B filing clean.

What is GST reconciliation?

GST reconciliation compares two invoice-level data sets: the purchases recorded in Tally or your purchase register, and the inward supplies your suppliers reported through GSTR-1 that appear in your GSTR-2B.

Any invoice missing from GSTR-2B can block ITC for that month. Any invoice present in GSTR-2B but absent from your books may indicate an unrecorded purchase. Both gaps should be reviewed before filing.

Why monthly reconciliation matters

ITC is now closely tied to supplier reporting in GSTR-2B. If a supplier delays filing GSTR-1, that invoice will not appear in your 2B and may not be claimable for that month.

Reconciling every month lets accountants follow up with suppliers early, claim ITC in the correct period, and avoid interest, penalties, and GST notices related to 2A/2B mismatches.

Steps to reconcile GSTR-2B with Tally

  • Export the purchase register from Tally for the relevant month in Excel.
  • Download GSTR-2B from the GST portal for the same return period.
  • Match invoices by supplier GSTIN and invoice number.
  • Review matched, missing-in-books, missing-in-2B, and tax mismatch rows.
  • Correct entries, follow up with suppliers, and finalise ITC before GSTR-3B.

Manual vs automated reconciliation

Manual VLOOKUP formulas work only for very small data sets. They often fail when column names differ, invoice numbers contain prefixes, dates are stored as text, or GSTINs include typing errors.

An automated GST reconciliation tool can auto-detect columns, normalise invoice numbers, flag GSTIN typos, and surface possible matches so minor data-entry differences do not hide eligible ITC.
