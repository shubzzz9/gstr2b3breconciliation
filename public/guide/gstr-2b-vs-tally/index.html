GSTR-2B vs Tally: How to Match Invoices

GSTR-2B is generated by the GST portal from supplier filings. Tally holds the purchase data entered by your team. The two rarely agree on the first try, so accurate matching rules are important.

Key differences between GSTR-2B and Tally

GSTR-2B is supplier-reported and becomes static after generation. Tally is buyer-recorded and editable at any time. This creates timing, formatting, and data-entry differences.

GSTR-2B uses invoice numbers exactly as suppliers filed them. Tally may store the same invoice with a series prefix, leading zeros, financial-year suffix, or a different date format.

Common reasons invoices do not match

  • Invoice number formatting: INV-001/24-25 in one file and 001 in another.
  • GSTIN typos: one wrong character makes the records appear as different suppliers.
  • Date mismatch: supplier reports March while the buyer books the purchase in April.
  • Tax difference: rounding, freight, discounts, or wrong HSN slab can change taxable value and tax.

Recommended matching logic

Match primarily on supplier GSTIN plus normalised invoice number. Use taxable value, IGST, CGST, and SGST as tie-breakers, not as the only matching key.

Near matches should be marked for review instead of discarded. For example, same GSTIN, similar invoice number, and same tax amount usually means the invoice deserves manual verification.
